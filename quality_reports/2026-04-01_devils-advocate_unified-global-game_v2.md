# Devil's Advocate Report — Unified Global Game Sketch v2

## Main vulnerability

The sketch is no longer easy to dismiss as a tautological conjunction of `A3` and `A4`. The stronger attack now is narrower: the global game seems to do most of the explanatory work on the autocratic side, while the democratic side still lives and dies mainly by `A5`. A skeptical referee can therefore argue that the manuscript has unified the informational shell but not fully unified the causal burden.

## Attacks by dimension

### Internal logic

1. The democratic side still has two mechanisms.
   - **Severity**: High
   - **Why it matters**: Under threshold automation, democracy already fails once `ell_1 = 0` and `A5` blocks fresh capacity in `t=2`. The high-noise coordination result then looks auxiliary rather than constitutive.
   - **How the author could respond**: State this explicitly and narrow the claim. Say the unified framework microfounds both sides, but the democratic result remains a timing-plus-credibility mechanism while the autocratic result is more purely coordination-driven.

2. `Proposition 0` is a theorem shell, not yet a theorem.
   - **Severity**: Medium
   - **Why it matters**: The existence of `hat{sigma}_x` now has assumptions and a proof sketch, but a hostile referee can still ask for a formal appendix proving continuity and the fixed-point mapping.
   - **How the author could respond**: Move the full derivation to an appendix and cite the exact monotone-comparative-statics theorem being used.

### Causal mechanism

1. The coalition club good `b_x` is still reduced-form.
   - **Severity**: Medium
   - **Why it matters**: The old fatal free-riding bug is gone, but `b_x` now carries a lot of behavioral content. A referee can ask why emergency transfers, legal support, or mutual protection are sufficiently excludable to discipline participation.
   - **How the author could respond**: Add one paragraph clarifying that the note only needs coalition membership to generate *some* excludable benefit, not a fully microfounded activist marketplace, and cite Olson-style selective incentives explicitly.

2. The mapping from automation trajectory to `sigma` remains the main reduced-form step.
   - **Severity**: Medium
   - **Why it matters**: The sketch is much cleaner now, but the core empirical bridge still says rapid shocks are publicly legible and threshold shocks are noisy. A critic can still ask why this is true in equilibrium rather than by narrative stipulation.
   - **How the author could respond**: Acknowledge openly that this is the remaining reduced-form bridge and frame it as the single residual assumption replacing two previous ones.

### Scope and generalization

1. The model may be more asymmetric than it first appears.
   - **Severity**: Medium
   - **Why it matters**: Democracy needs both an observed shock and a visible coalition, while autocracy only needs visibility to affect repression. That means the regimes do not process the same mediator in exactly symmetric ways.
   - **How the author could respond**: Embrace this as institutional asymmetry rather than pretending full formal symmetry.

## Ranking of vulnerabilities

1. Democratic fragility still leans mainly on `A5`, not on the global game.
2. `b_x` is still reduced-form, though no longer fatally so.
3. `hat{sigma}_x` is now defensible but still needs appendix-level proof.
4. The `trajectory -> sigma` bridge remains the main unresolved reduced-form assumption.

## What survives scrutiny

- The original “two disconnected appendices” critique is no longer decisive.
- The free-riding bug has been fixed in a serious way.
- The `sigma -> pi_x^* -> threshold` chain now has explicit assumptions and proof sketches.
- The exposition is now strong enough that a referee can disagree with the model, but not dismiss it as incoherent.
