# Editorial notes: AI Automation, Regime Type, and Crossed Fragility

**Original file**: /Users/manoelgaldino/Documents/DCP/Papers/IA-dem/paper.Rmd
**Date**: 2026-04-03

## Diagnosis of the original

The introduction communicates the argument well but has three structural problems: (1) it opens with a "We show" result statement rather than a puzzle or tension that draws the reader in; (2) the coordination mechanism is explained twice (paragraphs 1 and 5), creating redundancy; (3) excessive em dashes (5-6 instances) give the prose a fragmented, parenthetical texture that weakens the declarative force of the claims.

## Main changes

1. **Opening paragraph restructured**: replaced "We show that the same AI technology..." with a shorter, puzzle-oriented opening ("The political consequences of AI automation depend on timing") that motivates the contrast before stating the result. The finding now arrives at the end of the first paragraph after the reader understands why timing matters.

2. **"Imagine two scenarios in a near future where" removed**: replaced with the more direct "Consider the United States and China adopting the same AI." Dropped "Imagine" (slightly informal) and "in a near future where" (unnecessary temporal framing). Also dropped the parenthetical illustration "lawyers draft faster, accountants process more" to reduce em-dash-heavy prose.

3. **Illustration paragraph tightened**: the final two sentences now state the institutional asymmetry declaratively ("Democracies require a visible crisis to mobilize compensation; autocracies require the absence of organized opposition to maintain repression") instead of relying on nested parenthetical contrasts.

4. **"This paper fills that gap" eliminated**: generic throat-clearing. The gap statement (para 3) now ends with the specific absence ("No formal model connects the shape of economic disruption to regime-specific survival") and the next paragraph opens directly with the model description.

5. **Gap paragraph reorganized**: the order was inverted from the original. Now: stability literature → automation literature → gap. This follows a funnel structure: what each field does → what neither does.

6. **Mechanism explained once**: removed the duplicated explanation from the results paragraph (old para 5). The mechanism is now stated in the model paragraph (para 4: signal noise → coordination → institutional response) and the results paragraph (para 5) uses the mechanism implicitly without restating it from scratch.

7. **Em dashes eliminated**: from 5-6 down to zero. All replaced with commas, parentheses, or sentence restructuring. The prose reads cleaner and more controlled.

8. **"crossed fragility" italicized only once** (first use, para 1). The original used it with italics twice and with em-dash apposition once.

9. **Class compromise paragraph compressed**: from two sentences to one. The original said "the welfare state as institutional insurance formalizes a specific implication of that bargain (Section 4)," which is already captured in the new version's "when the democratic bargain is institutionalized as a standing welfare state, it insures the regime itself against technological disruption." Section reference removed (the roadmap paragraph handles navigation).

10. **Welfare results paragraph (old para 7) tightened**: removed the sentence "The welfare state achieves this resilience while compensating workers; repression achieves it while leaving them to bear the full cost" — this duplicates the content of Proposition 4 (already stated in the preceding sentence) and the welfare gap (stated immediately after).

## What was preserved

- The US/China illustration: effective, concrete, and specific to this paper. Kept nearly intact.
- The three-result structure (crossed fragility, welfare cost, institutional insurance): clear and appropriate for a formal theory paper.
- All citations: Acemoglu & Robinson, Przeworski, Gans & Goldfarb, Autor et al., Lake, Chwe, Kuran, Edmond, Przeworski & Wallerstein, Moene & Wallerstein.
- The roadmap paragraph: kept verbatim except minor cleanup ("both" removed before "democratic compensation").
- Technical content: $\sigma$, $\bar{\kappa}$, proposition numbers, all formal claims.

## Points for author verification

1. **Opening claim "The political consequences of AI automation depend on timing"**: this is a simplification. The original said "depending entirely on the automation trajectory." Trajectory is more precise than timing (trajectory includes the complementarity phase, not just the delay). The author may prefer "depend on the trajectory of job loss" or similar.

2. **Dropped "lawyers draft faster, accountants process more"**: this was a concrete illustration of the complementary phase. Its removal makes the threshold scenario slightly more abstract. The author may want it back if they feel the reader needs the example.

3. **"false security and disoriented loss"**: "false security" is the author's implicit characterization of the complementary phase. The author should verify this captures their intended framing. "Disoriented" is a characterization of the coordination failure — workers are not confused, they lack common knowledge. The author may prefer "uncoordinated loss."

4. **Class compromise sentence**: the rewrite says "when the democratic bargain is institutionalized as a standing welfare state, it insures the regime itself against technological disruption." The original was more precise about Section 4 as the location. The author should verify that the compressed version captures the intended connection.

5. **Roadmap**: kept nearly verbatim. If the author has reorganized sections since the last edit, verify the section numbers still match.
