# Rewritten abstract: AI Automation, Regime Type, and Crossed Fragility

**Original file**: paper.Rmd (YAML abstract block)
**Date**: 2026-03-28

---

The United States and China lead the AI revolution. One is a democracy; the other is an autocracy. We develop a formal model showing that the trajectory of AI automation, not its magnitude, determines which regime type is more fragile. We compare two trajectories from the O-Ring automation framework (Gans and Goldfarb 2026): rapid displacement, where workers lose jobs immediately, and threshold automation, where AI initially complements labor and later substitutes it. We find a crossed fragility result: democracies are stable under rapid displacement but fragile under threshold automation, while autocracies exhibit the reverse. The mechanism is the coordination capacity of affected workers. Rapid shocks generate common knowledge of grievance, enabling organized opposition that democracies channel into compensation but that degrades autocratic repression. Threshold shocks produce strategic uncertainty that prevents coordination, paralyzing democratic response while preserving repressive effectiveness. We show that comprehensive social insurance achieves the same institutional resilience as repressive capacity, with strictly superior welfare outcomes. The model generates a five-type regime typology with testable predictions about which countries are most vulnerable to which automation trajectory.
