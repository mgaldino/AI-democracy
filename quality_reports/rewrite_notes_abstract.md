# Editorial notes: Abstract rewrite

**Original file**: paper.Rmd (YAML abstract block)
**Date**: 2026-03-28

## Diagnosis of the original

The original abstract is substantively complete but has three presentation issues. First, it opens with a generic rhetorical question ("How does AI-driven automation affect...") rather than a concrete fact. Second, two em dashes create the programmatic cadence flagged by the style guide. Third, the phrase "not just its magnitude" is a contrastive formula, and "We further show" is metadiscourse.

## Main changes

1. **Opening replaced.** Rhetorical question → concrete fact ("The United States and China lead the AI revolution. One is a democracy; the other is an autocracy."). Mirrors the intro opening. Anchors the reader immediately.

2. **Em dashes eliminated.** "not just its magnitude" → "not its magnitude" (direct). "without the human cost" → "with strictly superior welfare outcomes" (precise, no dash needed).

3. **"We further show" removed.** Direct statement instead of metadiscourse.

4. **Trajectory definitions inlined.** Instead of parenthetical "(initial complementarity followed by sudden substitution)", uses a relative clause: "where AI initially complements labor and later substitutes it." More readable.

5. **"clear empirical mapping" trimmed.** Removed "clear" (upgrade word). Kept "testable predictions."

6. **"Welfare states function as the democratic equivalent of standing repressive apparatus" removed.** This metaphor appears in the introduction; repeating it in the abstract is redundant. Replaced with the precise claim: "same institutional resilience, strictly superior welfare outcomes."

- All formal content: crossed fragility, coordination mechanism, welfare state result, typology
